How Cart Keeper Works

Cart Keeper is a small browser extension for saving products from online stores into one local list.

What it saves

When Cart Keeper recognizes a product page, it can save the product name, price, currency, image, product link, store name, store link, and the last save date. Saved products stay in your browser storage.

How products are detected

Cart Keeper tries to understand the current page in three ways:

  1. Structured product data. First, it looks for schema.org product data with an offer. Many stores publish this data so browsers and search engines can understand that the page is a product page.
  2. Supported store integrations. If structured data is not available, Cart Keeper checks store-specific integrations, such as Trodo, where the page layout is known and can be read reliably.
  3. Generic product signals. If there is no structured data or supported integration, Cart Keeper looks for strong product-page signals such as a product title, price, image, and buy/add-to-cart controls.

How to use it

  • Open a product page in a store.
  • Open Cart Keeper from the browser toolbar.
  • If the product can be detected, press Add.
  • If the product is already saved, press Update to refresh its data.

Privacy

Cart Keeper does not send saved products to external servers. You can read more in the Privacy Policy.
